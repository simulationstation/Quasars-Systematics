Core Findings

  • The dipole amplitude remains near D ≈ 1.6 × 10-2 across faint limits, but the axis drifts strongly with depth.
  • The axis shift from near-CMB alignment to about 34° at faint cuts is driven by growth in the CMB-perpendicular component.
  • Clustered mock catalogs preserve high nominal amplitude S/N, but do not remove axis-instability concerns.
  • Injection-recovery tests show depth-linked completeness modulation can bias both dipole amplitude and axis if model templates are misspecified.
  • Correlated-cut Monte Carlo tests place the observed drift span in a <1% tail under a Poisson-only null.
  • Epoch-sliced CatWISE-parent amplitudes show large analytic constant-D statistics (χ²=308.2 dipole-only; χ²=51.0 minimal nuisance; χ²=52.7 richer nuisance).
  • A constrained rich parametric bootstrap with a shared dipole vector gives P(rangesim ≥ rangeobs)=0.974 and P(χ²sim ≥ χ²obs)=0.983, so observed epoch variability is expected under constant dipole + measured epoch-dependent systematics.
  • Attribution is ranked by leave-one-template-out: dropping cos λecl shifts Δχ²=+22.4, then stellar-count (+6.5), sin λecl (+4.4), and depth (+3.6).
  • At W1max=16.6, a maximal-nuisance suite with explicit low-ℓ modes shows the free-axis solution is nuisance-subspace sensitive; a CMB-parallel constrained-null bootstrap gives pabs=P(|D∥,sim| ≥ |D∥,obs|)=0.707 for D∥,true=0.0046.

Dark-Siren Results (Unrelated to Quasar Dipole)

The following analysis represents an independent research vector, distinct from the primary quasar-dipole study. We are presenting these results as a standalone component to establish research priority and ensure the timely dissemination of these specific findings.

While the Quasar Dipole study identifies a likely systematic origin, this separate audit of the GWTC-3 catalog uncovers a persistent statistical tension that survives standard null tests.

Dark-Siren Findings (GWTC-3)

From A robust calibrated dark-siren tension with General-Relativity propagation in GWTC-3.

In plain terms: Under this fixed scoring framework, the observed dataset is approximately 39× more likely under the modified-propagation model than under the GR baseline.

  • Using 36 GWTC-3 dark sirens, the modified-propagation model yields ΔLPDtot = +3.670 versus GR.
  • In 512 matched GR-null injections, the score distribution is centered at -0.839 with σ = 0.240, has maximum +0.076, and has 0/512 runs with ΔLPD ≥ 3.
  • Mechanism controls localize most of the signal to the distance-redshift/selection channel: distance-only weighting retains ΔLPD ≈ +2.995, while sky-only weighting is subdominant at ≈ +0.969.
  • Fixed-power response and a nine-variant systematics matrix shift scores but do not reproduce the observed high-amplitude signal under tested GR-consistent nuisance variations.
  • The result is presented as a calibrated anomaly relative to the tested GR null, with interpretation bounded by potentially unmodeled selection and catalog systematics.

Hubble Tension Implications

From The Hubble Tension as an Inference-Bias Signal: Dark Sirens with Modified Gravitational-Wave Propagation.

In plain terms: If the GWTC-3 propagation anomaly is physical, analysing it with a GR standard ruler can bias inferred H0 high by ~2-5 km s-1 Mpc-1, enough to mimic a substantial part of the Hubble tension.

  • Planck-facing MG recalibration gives H0Planck,MG ≈ 68.0 with ΩmPlanck,MG ≈ 0.306.
  • GR standard-ruler inversion of MG-consistent draws yields mean ΔH0 ≈ +1.9 (fixed Ωm) to +4.6 km s-1 Mpc-1 (lensing-proxy Ωm).
  • Baseline CMB-lensing projection is suppressed, but an MG-aware response refit restores near-reference fit quality (median χ² ≈ 8.1 versus 9.0 for the Planck reference).
  • A propagation-amplitude dial finds core cross-probe consistency near α ≈ 0.6, while material closure at 0.30 would require α ≈ 2 under linear coupling (scale estimate).
  • Dark-siren robustness package: bounded selection-function and completeness deformations do not erase the preference (adversarial minimum ΔLPD ≈ +3.67 across the tested nuisance family).
  • Host-model stress tests: monotonic luminosity reweightings plus aggressive redshift-dependent tilts and a crude completeness upweight reduce but do not erase the preference (ΔLPD ≳ +3.18, i.e. exp(ΔLPD) ≳ 24×).
  • Catalogue/photo-z and PE stresses: extreme photo-z biases reduce but do not erase the preference (ΔLPD ≈ +1.98 minimum on the tested grid), and PE analysis-group swaps keep ΔLPD ≈ +3.63. The preference concentrates at higher distance/redshift proxies.
  • Spec-z override legitness suite: crossmatching the top-weight host candidates against SDSS DR16 + public spec-z catalogues anchors ~4%, ~7%, and ~10% of total host-weight proxy (three events) to spectroscopic redshifts at r ≤ 30″; the score is stable/slightly higher (ΔLPD ≈ 3.63 → 3.65) and non-decreasing with spec-z coverage (pooled slope ≈ 0.0035 in ΔLPD per +1% coverage), persisting under strict match-quality cuts. A shifted-sky control shows 60″ matches are coincidence-dominated.
  • Redshift-scramble null (spectral-only, binned-z approximation): observed ΔLPDdata ≈ +2.65 versus a scramble mean +2.27 ± 0.13, with empirical tail probability p ≈ 0.025 (total p ≈ 0.05 including selection).
