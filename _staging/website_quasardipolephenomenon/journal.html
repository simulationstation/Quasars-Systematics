On the Identifiability of Negative-Binomial Counts-in-Cells Radio Dipole Fits Under Survey Systematics Templates

15 February 2026 • Paper release • Radio dipole identifiability / negative-binomial counts-in-cells

This note audits a negative-binomial (NB) counts-in-cells dipole estimator for wide-area radio catalogs by reproducing dipole-only fits and then stress-testing them with survey-systematics templates. On a joint three-survey (LoTSS+RACS+NVSS) fit, an exponential modulation model with physically motivated templates yields a large likelihood gain and is favored even under BIC (ΔBIC ≈ −106.8), while injection/recovery shows that modest structured selection can drive large axis mis-recovery in the dipole-only model (median axis error ≈ 63° in one configuration).

Paper

Manuscript (PRD / REVTeX note): PDF · TeX.

Software + run artefacts (Zenodo): 10.5281/zenodo.18719277. Parent archive: 10.5281/zenodo.18476711.

What Was Tested

  • Question: is a dipole-only NB counts-in-cells model identifiable on real radio survey footprints?
  • Data: publicly staged NVSS (1.4 GHz), RACS-low DR1 (888 MHz), and LoTSS-DR2 (144 MHz) catalogs.
  • Footprints: geometrically defined supports including zero-count HEALPix cells (Nside=32), so map support is footprint-defined rather than data-defined.
  • Stress tests: add nuisance templates (geometry + physical proxies) and compare fit quality and recovered direction stability.

Model Variants

  • Pure: dipole-only modulation.
  • Exp(Dec/RA): positivity-preserving exponential modulation with basic geometric templates (δ, δ², sin α, cos α).
  • Exp(+phys): adds physical proxies (Haslam 408 MHz; NVSS flux-uncertainty proxy; LoTSS RMS/mask/MOC-coverage proxies).

Headline Results (run snapshot)

  • Three-survey joint fit: Exp(+phys) improves log-likelihood by Δlogℒ ≈ +185.17 and is BIC-preferred (ΔBIC ≈ −106.77 vs pure).
  • Direction instability: adding nuisance freedom produces large direction shifts relative to the dipole-only solution.
  • Injection/recovery: in one configuration (n=80 sims), the pure model’s median axis error is 63.4°, while the template-augmented model recovers the injected axis (median error 0°).

Assessing a Candidate Modified Gravity Anomaly in GWTC-3 Dark Siren Propagation

14 February 2026 • Paper release (updated 20 February 2026) • Dark-siren propagation anomaly (GWTC-3 / O3)

We test whether the GWTC-3 O3 dark-siren catalogue prefers a non-General-Relativity mapping between observed GW amplitude and luminosity distance by scoring 3D GW localisations against 3D galaxy-catalogue structure (with an out-of-catalog completion and injection-calibrated selection normalisation). In this fixed scoring framework, a reference modified-propagation history yields a calibrated predictive-score shift of ΔLPD_tot = +3.670 (≈ 39× in exp(ΔLPD)) relative to an internal GR propagation baseline, and the preference is dominated by the data term (ΔLPD_data = +2.670) rather than the selection term (ΔLPD_sel = +1.000). However, an identifiability control that forces GR and modified hypotheses to compete on shared catalogue support in a masked anchored-only construction removes the modified-propagation data-term advantage (ΔLPD_tot = 0.686, ΔLPD_data = −0.582), indicating sensitivity to catalogue support/normalisation. A membership-changing rotation-baseline contrast remains positive (ΔC_cat,data = +1.849, p ≈ 0.007), demonstrating non-trivial sky–catalogue association.

What Was Tested

  • Question: does GWTC-3 dark-siren data prefer a fixed modified-propagation mapping over an internal GR propagation baseline?
  • Data: 36 O3 dark sirens (GWTC-3), scored with a galaxy-catalogue likelihood marginalising host incompleteness.
  • Selection control: injection-calibrated selection normalisation separated into ΔLPD_data and ΔLPD_sel.
  • Evolution discriminant (partial): dominance of ΔLPD_data over ΔLPD_sel disfavors a purely isotropic rate/selection shift, but does not by itself uniquely identify propagation physics.
  • Host-model stresses: monotonic luminosity-weight families plus redshift-tilt and completeness-upweight stress tests reduce but do not erase the preference (adversarial minimum ΔLPD_tot ≳ +3.18 across the tested set).
  • Spec-z anchoring audit: override a growing fraction of top host-weight candidates with external spectroscopy (public spec-z catalogs + SDSS/DESI), with shifted-sky false-match controls; the preference does not weaken under increased anchoring.
  • Overdensity/contrast check: subtract a membership-changing random-rotation baseline from the top-event catalogue term; the contrast remains positive (ΔC_cat,data = +1.849) with rotation-null p ≈ 0.007.
  • Proxy-matched patch null (contrast): a reviewer-clean conditional null (proxy-matched on low-|b|, extinction, stellar density, depth/spec-z proxies) applied directly to the shared-support contrast gives Δcontrast ≈ 0.456 with leave-one-out p ≈ 0.072 for GW200208_130117 (rebuilt per-patch spec-z subset from 6dFGS/2MRS/PSCz; rejection-sampled to 1000 finite reps; accept_rate ≈ 0.81).
  • Identifiability controls: a masked anchored-only shared-support diagnostic (GR/MG forced to share identical catalogue support) removes the modified-propagation advantage (ΔLPD_data = −0.582), while a membership-preserving radial destroyer (permuting dL_EM(z) across in-region galaxies) collapses the masked anchored-only score relative to observation.
  • Dominant-event patch audit: a dedicated sky-patch forensic audit for a near-plane high-leverage event (GW200208_130117) using membership-changing RA shifts and matched nulls conditioned on low-|b|, host-weight depth proxies, SFD E(B–V) (mean/CV/max), and Gaia DR3 star density (G<18 total/CV); no obvious extinction/star-density discontinuity is identified.
  • Calibration: matched GR-truth injection catalogues are used to characterise the false-alarm behaviour under the tested generator.

Headline Numbers (paper snapshot)

  • Observed score shift: ΔLPD_tot = +3.670 (ΔLPD_data = +2.670, ΔLPD_sel = +1.000).
  • Masked anchored-only (full likelihood): ΔLPD_tot = 3.455, ΔLPD_data = 2.419 (top events’ in-catalogue term restricted to supported spec-z overrides).
  • Shared-support diagnostic (masked anchored-only): ΔLPD_tot = 0.686, ΔLPD_data = −0.582 (MG advantage removed when support is forced shared).
  • Radial destroyer (masked anchored-only): dL_EM-permutation gives mean ΔLPD_data ≈ 1.220 over N=2000 replicates, with p(data ≥ obs)=0.
  • GR-truth injection calibration: 512 catalogues give mean −0.839 with maximum +0.076 (no GR-truth replicate approaches the observed scale).
  • Host-model stress range: across z-dependent tilts / completeness-upweights, ΔLPD_tot ≈ 3.18–4.16 (data-term dominated).
  • All-out spec-z override (public): in the spectral-only+selection channel, baseline ΔLPD_tot = 3.634 rises to 3.684 at r=30″, K=100000; at r=30″, K=200000 the median anchored host-weight proxy across the five high-leverage events is 11.8% (with r=60″ excluded by shifted-sky controls).
  • Overdensity/contrast: top-event catalogue-term contrast (true minus membership-changing random rotations) ΔC_cat,data = +1.849, rotation-null p ≈ 0.007.
  • Proxy-matched contrast null (GW200208_130117): shared-support contrast under a proxy-matched patch ensemble (rebuilding a per-patch spec-z subset from 6dFGS/2MRS/PSCz) gives Δcontrast ≈ 0.456 with leave-one-out p ≈ 0.072 (1000 finite reps via rejection sampling; accept_rate ≈ 0.81; candidate pool 484 proxy-matched patches).
  • Patch audit (GW200208_130117): matched null p-values: p(⟨E(B–V)⟩ ≥ event) = 0.194, p(N★ ≥ event) = 0.215 (Gaia DR3 G<18); top-1 contributes 0.14% of total w×p_sky across 11255 in-patch candidates.
  • Interpretation: a calibrated, sky-associated catalogue-scoring anomaly consistent with (but not uniquely identified as) modified propagation; shared-support controls indicate substantial sensitivity to catalogue support/normalisation.

Causal-Closure Test: Ruler Mismatch Can Mimic a Dark-Siren Anomaly

14 February 2026 • Causal-closure test (dark sirens) • implications for catalogue dipoles

This entry documents a causal-closure experiment inside the O3 dark-siren pipeline. The purpose is narrow and technical: if the modified-propagation history used in the Hubble-tension paper is the truth, does a GR-baseline analysis naturally generate a dark-siren score excursion on the observed scale (ΔLPD ≈ 3.67)?

Show full entry

Why quasar dipoles are mentioned here: once a pipeline is shown to generate a tension-scale “anomaly” from a ruler mismatch (truth ≠ analysis assumption), the default interpretation of other high-tension catalog signals changes. In particular, anisotropy measurements that depend on catalogue modelling, selection functions, and distance/redshift compression can exhibit dipole-like artefacts under misspecification. This entry does not claim an end-to-end simulation of the quasar dipole; it records a mechanism demonstration and its implications for catalogue-driven dipole claims.

What Was Run

  • Forward model (truth): generate synthetic dark-siren catalogs under MG-truth propagation using the fixed R(z) template used in the Hubble-tension analysis.
  • Analysis model: score each synthetic catalog with the same ΔLPD definition and selection normalisation as the O3 dark-siren headline result.
  • Controls: (i) GR-truth forward catalogs; (ii) MG-truth with the catalog structure removed (toy uniform-z weighting) to test whether the score scale depends on catalog geometry.

Headline Outcome

  • Observed (real O3 data): ΔLPD_total = 3.669945.
  • MG-truth forward catalogs (256 reps): mean 3.504, median 3.442, and P(ΔLPD_total ≥ 3.669945) = 0.219.
  • GR-truth forward catalogs (256 reps): mean 3.313, median 3.309, and P(ΔLPD_total ≥ 3.669945) = 0.0195.
  • MG-truth with catalog removed (256 reps): mean 0.637 (the large-score scale collapses under deliberately structureless z-weighting).

In plain terms: under this forward model, ΔLPD ≈ 3.67 is much more typical if the MG propagation template is true than if GR propagation is true, and the score amplitude depends strongly on the catalogue/likelihood structure rather than being “free”.

Interpretation: this is a causal-closure / mechanism-alignment check, not a GR-null. The strict GR-null calibration for the production score is the matched injection suite reported in the main dark-siren paper.

A key strengthening check is a dose–response dial in the forward generator, Rα(z) = 1 + α [R(z) − 1]: the tail probability P(ΔLPD_total ≥ 3.669945) rises monotonically with α (from 0.023 at α=0 to 0.219 at α=1 in the run snapshot below).

Implications for Dipole Analyses

The quasar dipole analysis on the front page shows a characteristic “artifact-like” signature: the amplitude remains high while the axis drifts with depth. In practice, this is the sort of behaviour that can be produced when an analysis pipeline compresses real sky-dependent structure through a misspecified modelling assumption (for example: an incorrect selection template, completeness model, or distance/redshift ruler).

The causal-closure result above does not directly simulate the quasar dipole pipeline, but it does demonstrate a concrete mechanism: a ruler mismatch can generate a tension-scale “anomaly” of the same order as the observed dark-siren ΔLPD once it is filtered through catalogue and selection machinery. That is why we treat dipole-scale claims as “pipeline outputs” rather than direct observables, and why we focus on forward-model closure tests and structure-destruction nulls.

Caution: this is a mechanism demonstration, not a proof that any specific quasar-dipole measurement is an artefact.

Testing the Bekenstein-Hawking Area Law at Cosmological Scales: A Data-Driven Reconstruction of the Horizon Entropy Current

12 February 2026 • Paper release • Horizon thermodynamics / entropy-current reconstruction

The Bekenstein-Hawking area law implies a constant horizon entropy slope, μ(A) ∝ dS/dA = const. This paper tests that assumption directly at cosmological scales using a data-driven reconstruction of the apparent-horizon entropy current from late-time background geometry (Pantheon+, BAO, and cosmic chronometers), in a framework designed to avoid GR-anchored circularity. The reconstruction prefers a time-evolving entropy slope with a negative mean gradient, ⟨d log μ / d log A⟩ ≈ −0.24, with posterior weight P(d log μ / d log A < 0) ≈ 0.79.

Show full entry

Why This Matters

The same thermodynamic degree of freedom that governs horizon entropy also controls modified gravitational-wave propagation (a “friction” effect) in dark-siren analyses. If μ(A) evolves, it provides a concrete bridge between background geometry and multimessenger propagation tests.

What Was Done

  • Null hypothesis: strict Bekenstein-Hawking behavior on cosmological apparent horizons (μ(A)=1).
  • Inference strategy: forward-model a spline representation of log μ, solve for H(z), and evaluate the likelihood in data space.
  • Data stack: Pantheon+, BAO, and cosmic chronometers.
  • Calibration layer: synthetic closure and simulation-based calibration checks to guard against reconstruction artifacts.

Key Results (paper snapshot)

  • Negative-slope preference: ⟨d log μ / d log A⟩ ≈ −0.24 with P(d log μ / d log A < 0) ≈ 0.79.
  • Function-space proximity: non-extensive templates (Tsallis/Barrow-like) are substantially closer to the reconstructed μ(A) than μ=1 (representative distances D² ~ 10⁻⁸ vs 10⁻⁴).
  • Interpretation: supports an effective thermodynamic sector that evolves with horizon area, consistent with late-time modified-gravity channels that also impact siren propagation.
